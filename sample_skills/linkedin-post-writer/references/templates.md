# Post Templates (A–E)

All templates follow the Epiphany Bridge structure unless noted. All end with a P.S. question.

---

## Formatting Mechanics

**The staircase rule**: arrange lists so line length flows naturally. Three patterns:
- Shortest → longest (builds momentum)
- Longest → shortest (inverted, punchy ending)
- Mountain (short → long → short, emphasis in the middle)

Don't dump bullet points of the same length — visual variation makes content easier to scan.

**Parentheses as surprise factor**: use parentheses for asides that add context, a reveal, or a number that would be stronger separated: "(and it only took 2 hours)" or "(we've tested this with 40+ agencies)". Creates intimacy and specificity without breaking sentence rhythm.

**Every line must add information**: no filler emphasis lines. "They're wrong." with nothing added is a wasted line. Every sentence earns its place by adding a fact, a contrast, a number, or a reveal. Never use a line just to create drama.

**The "gist not full recipe" rule for How-To posts**: give direction and structure, not the entire operating manual. The reader should think "this person gets it" and still need you to execute. If you give everything away, there's no reason to hire you or come back. Give the framework, not the playbook.

**Shallow Idea / Targeted Platitude format**: low depth, high relevance. A statement that resonates only with your specific ICP — not generic inspiration. The goal: someone outside your ICP sees it and scrolls past; someone inside your ICP stops and clicks your profile. It's not about making everyone agree — it's about making the right person feel seen.

## Carousel Slide Architecture

**Specs**: 1080×1350px (4:5 portrait — takes up maximum feed space on LinkedIn). PDF format — each page = one swipeable slide. Upload as a document post.

**Slide 1 — Hook Slide**:
- Bold headline: 4–10 words max
- Optional subline under 10 words
- "Swipe →" indicator
- High contrast (dark background, white text or bold color)
- One idea, no clutter

**Content Slides (middle)**:
- ONE point per slide — never two ideas on one slide
- Headline: 3–8 words (the core takeaway)
- Supporting text: 1–2 short sentences only if needed
- Numbered if step-by-step ("Step 1", "Step 2")
- Generous whitespace
- Slide counter in corner (e.g., "3/9")

**Final Slide — CTA**:
- If lead magnet: "Comment '[KEYWORD]' and I'll DM it to you."
- If follow: "Follow [name] for more [topic]"
- Visually distinct (different background or inverted colors)
- One action, no clutter

**Typography**: Bold sans-serif for headlines. Lighter weight for supporting text. High contrast, readable at mobile size. Same font, same layout structure, same alignment across all slides.

**Caption rules for carousel posts**: Hook (3 lines, 4–10 words each) → teaser body (2–4 lines, doesn't repeat slide content word-for-word) → CTA matching the final slide. Short — the carousel does the heavy lifting.

**Use for**: AI systems, tech stacks, industry analysis, "here's how X works"  
**Length**: 2500–2900 chars  
**Voice**: Authoritative, educational, specific

```
[HOOK — bold claim + specific number]

[1–2 lines of setup/context]

Here's what most [role/industry] miss:

[Point 1 — specific, named]
[Point 2]
[Point 3]
...up to 7–8 points

[Synthesis line — the "so what"]

[CTA — comment gate or save prompt]

P.S. [Question that invites the reader to share their experience]
```

**Example hook**: *"We processed 4,000 influencer profiles in 90 minutes. A week ago that took 3 analysts."*

## Template B — Listicle Framework
**Use for**: Lessons learned, principles, observations from experience  
**Length**: 2000–2500 chars  
**Voice**: Reflective, earned authority

```
[HOOK — number + outcome or contrarian]

[1–2 lines framing what earned these lessons]

[Numbered list, 5–9 items]
Each item: bold label + 1–3 lines of explanation
Use specifics, not platitudes

[Closing line — zoom out to the bigger truth]

[CTA]

P.S. [Question]
```

## Template C — Case Study / Before-After
**Use for**: Client transformations, campaign results, AI deployment outcomes  
**Length**: 2000–2500 chars  
**Voice**: Storyteller, specific, credible

```
[HOOK — the result first, then the setup]

[The "before" — paint the problem with specifics]

[The turning point — what changed, what decision was made]

[The "after" — concrete numbers, named outcomes]

[What this means for the industry / broader takeaway]

[CTA]

P.S. [Question]
```

**Note**: Never name clients without permission. Use "a luxury beauty brand" or "a Paris-based fashion house."

## Template D — Comment-Gated Lead Magnet
**Use for**: Maximum engagement, resource giveaways, building email list  
**Length**: 1500–2000 chars  
**Voice**: Direct, generous, high-value teaser

```
[HOOK — what the resource delivers + specific number]

[2–3 lines on WHY this resource exists / what problem it solves]

[3–5 bullet teaser of what's inside]

[The gate]
Comment "[KEYWORD]" and I'll send it directly.

[Social proof line if available — "X people already asked for this"]

[Follow prompt]

P.S. [Question related to the topic]
```

## CTA Frameworks by Goal

### Lead Magnet (highest converting)
```
Connect with me first (so I can DM you), then comment "[WORD]" and I'll send it.
```
- Comment "SYSTEM" and I'll send the playbook.
- Drop "AUDIT" below and I'll send the framework.
- Comment "SEND IT" and I'll share the templates.

**Why connect first**: You can't DM someone you're not connected with. By making the connection the first step, every commenter becomes a connection. Every connection becomes a follower. 1,000 comments = 1,000 new connections = 1,000 new followers. Always include this instruction.

**Repost bonus (optional)**: "Repost for priority access." Whether you send it faster or not, every repost exposes your lead magnet to that person's entire network. Geometric reach expansion.

**Top keywords**: single-word nouns — SYSTEM, GUIDE, PLAYBOOK, AUDIT, TEMPLATE, SCRIPT, DEMO, MEETINGS, CLAUDE

### Conversation Starter (drives comments)
```
What [tool/system/method] do you use for [topic]? Drop it below.
```
- What tool do you use for prospect research? Comment it below.
- What's your actual reply rate on LinkedIn DMs right now?
- What method do you use for follow-ups?

### Authority (debate-driving)
```
Agree? Disagree? Let me know below.
```
- Hot take or common sense? Tell me below.
- Am I wrong? Change my mind.
- What would you add to this?

### Share (amplification)
```
Repost this if you know someone who needs it.
```
Use sparingly — algorithm de-prioritizes explicit share requests if overused.

## Template E — Conversational Story
**Use for**: Personal leadership moments, failures, pivots, vulnerability  
**Length**: 1800–2500 chars  
**Voice**: Human, honest, specific — no corporate gloss

```
[HOOK — the moment, not the lesson]

[Scene-setting — where you were, what was happening]
Use short sentences. Present tense if possible.

[The problem / tension / mistake]

[The realization — the "epiphany"]

[What changed + what it produced]

[The universal lesson — written for the reader]

[CTA — often a question or save prompt]

P.S. [Question that opens dialogue]
```

**S-Tier patterns (highest engagement)**: vulnerability/failure stories, European/luxury angle + revenue transparency
